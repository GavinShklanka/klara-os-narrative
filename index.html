Section 01 — The Crisis

The numbers don't
lie quietly.

In 2023–24, Nova Scotia's emergency departments recorded over half a million visits. More than a third were non-urgent — patients who ended up in the wrong part of the system. Over 52,000 left without ever being seen.

◎ The missing layer
Nova Scotia's healthcare infrastructure is not the problem. The system lacks a decision layer that routes patients to the right care stream before they default to emergency departments.

KLARA introduces this missing navigation layer by guiding patients toward appropriate services such as pharmacies, virtual care, and primary care networks. This improves access to care while protecting emergency capacity.

Section 04 — System Architecture

Four layers.
One operating system.

KLARA OS is not an application — it is a middleware layer that integrates with every major component of Nova Scotia's healthcare infrastructure.

01
User Interaction Layer
Patient-facing intake via YourHealthNS app, web portal, or 811 digital pathway.

Supports app, web, and 811-integrated voice pathways. The voice pathway is critical for rural and elderly demographics — the discontinued MyHealthNS portal (shut down 2020 due to low uptake) is a warning against app-only strategies. YourHealthNS "Nova" AI assistant becomes the primary entry point with structured intake and risk-stratified routing.

YourHealthNS811 VoiceWeb PortalAccessibility
02
Navigation Intelligence Engine
Symptom parsing, risk classification, and RAG-powered clinical reasoning.

Modular RAG (Retrieval Augmented Generation) architecture — recommended by 2025 evidence as the best combination of accuracy, traceability, and safety for clinical applications. Schmitt-Thompson protocol alignment ensures industry-standard triage logic. SNOMED-CT mapping enables pan-Canadian interoperability. Non-diagnostic constraint is architecturally enforced.

RAGSNOMED-CTSchmitt-ThompsonNon-Diagnostic
03
Healthcare System Integration Layer
FHIR R4 interoperability with OPOR, VirtualCareNS, Novari, DIS/SHARE, EMRs, AI Scribe.

Oracle Health (OPOR) provides FHIR R4 APIs, SMART on FHIR app launch, and CDS Hooks — confirmed on the Millennium platform. EMR integration with TELUS Med Access and QHR Accuro via FHIR DocumentReference or Ocean eReferrals. Novari ($17M/5yr) handles downstream referral management — KLARA feeds pre-qualified, structured referrals into Novari eRequest.

FHIR R4SMART on FHIROPORNovariOcean
04
Provincial Healthcare Infrastructure
Capacity data feeds, community ED closure status, rural load-balancing.

The capacity layer is the most challenging — no unified real-time service API exists today. Routing uses weighted scoring: proximity × availability × acuity-match × patient-mobility. Hard constraints: no routing to closed facilities, no exceeding panel caps, mandatory escalation for Red cases. FHIR R4 scheduling resources provide the data model.

Section 05 — Ecosystem Activation

The infrastructure exists.
The delivery doesn't.

Nova Scotia has invested $365M in OPOR, $17M in Novari, and ongoing funding for VirtualCareNS, Virtual Hallway, and AI Scribe. These systems work. What's missing is the upstream navigation layer that connects patients to them. KLARA doesn't replace anything — it activates everything.

⬟
OPOR (One Person One Record)
$365M · Oracle Health
Province-wide clinical information system replacing 80+ legacy apps. IWK live Dec 2025; Central Zone May 2026.
Gap: No patient-facing intake intelligence. Receives patients without pre-structured data.
+ KLARA: Delivers FHIR R4 structured intake packets so clinicians see organized data on first encounter.
💻
VirtualCareNS
Maple Platform · 108K registered
Virtual primary care — 150–200 appointments/day. 154,823 visits to March 2024. Turns away 100–150 patients daily.
Gap: No intelligent triage. First-come-first-served. Capacity-constrained.
+ KLARA: Pre-screens patients, routes appropriate cases with structured intake, reduces turn-aways.
🔗
Virtual Hallway
1,016 providers · 50+ specialties
Provider-to-provider phone consults. 84% negate in-person specialist referral.
Gap: Provider-initiated only. Patients can't trigger it. Not connected to navigation.
+ KLARA: Identifies cases where PCP + Virtual Hallway consult may resolve the issue and flags this pathway.
💊
DIS / SHARE
Provincial medication system
Province-wide medication profiles from all community pharmacies since 2016. SHARE aggregates hospital, lab, DI data.
Gap: View-only for clinicians. Not connected to navigation decisions. Looked up reactively, not proactively.
+ KLARA: Queries DIS/SHARE to enrich routing — flags complex medication profiles for higher-acuity pathways.
📋
Novari
$17M/5yr · Referral management
MIRM, eRequest, MHA mental health intake. Province-wide Dec 2025.
Gap: Manages referrals after creation. Doesn't help patients decide whether or where to seek care.
+ KLARA: Feeds pre-qualified structured referrals into Novari eRequest, reducing rejections and rework.
🤖
AI Scribe + KLARA
End-to-end admin reduction
AI Scribe handles in-encounter documentation (Mikata Health, Infoway-funded). KLARA handles pre-encounter intake.
Gap: AI Scribe only addresses during/after encounter. Pre-encounter intake remains manual and redundant.
+ Combined: KLARA pre-encounter + AI Scribe in-encounter = estimated 30–50% total admin reduction.
KLARA is the activation layer.
The province has built the roads. KLARA is the GPS. Without upstream navigation, OPOR receives disorganized intake, VirtualCareNS turns away patients the ED could serve, and Virtual Hallway remains invisible to those who need it most.

Section 07 — Trust & Infrastructure

Governance is
non-negotiable.

Navigation intelligence — not diagnostic authority. Every decision auditable, every override mandatory, every byte on Canadian soil.

🔒
PHIA
Personal Health Information Act
Nova Scotia's PHIA requires mandatory Record of User Activity for any system maintaining personal health information. KLARA operates under a custodian (NS Health) with full breach notification and consent management. PIA required before data access.
🛡
PIPEDA
Federal Privacy Legislation
Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act — federal privacy alignment. KLARA's PHIA compliance exceeds PIPEDA requirements. Follows principles of consent, limited collection, accuracy, safeguards.
⬡
FHIR R4
Healthcare Interoperability
Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources, Release 4 — HL7 international standard. KLARA builds to CA Core+ profile set (v0.3.0), aligned with pan-Canadian interoperability roadmap targeting 2027 completion.
🇨🇦
Data Residency
Canadian Infrastructure
PIIDPA (Personal Information International Disclosure and Protection Act) restricts international transfer. KLARA is hosted exclusively on Canadian infrastructure. No patient data crosses national boundaries.
📋
Audit Logging
Tamper-Evident Logs
Every routing decision logged: timestamp, signal weights, confidence scores, RAG retrieval sources, patient actions. Tamper-evident logging exceeds PIPEDA requirements. Full audit trail for health authority administrators.
